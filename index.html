Executive Summary

Malaysia classifies aluminium slag as Scheduled Waste (SW 104) and lacks clear reuse policy for steel slag. This proposal recommends a feasibility study and policy reform, benchmarking Taiwan’s successful circular economy model.

Problem Statement

Malaysia Taiwan
Slag = Scheduled Waste Slag = Industrial Byproduct
No domestic treatment facilities Licensed recycling tech
No downstream market Reuse in cement/construction
Risk-averse regulation Circular Economy Law

Proposal Objectives

  • 🧪 Evaluate practices in Sarawak
  • 🌍 Benchmark Taiwan & global models
  • 📊 Assess feasibility (env + econ)
  • 🧭 Develop SOPs and policy roadmap

Benefits of Approval

♻️ Recover valuable aluminium & steel
🌱 Reduce landfill impact
📉 Lower emissions & costs
🧱 Supply cement/construction industry
📑 Align with PCDS 2030 goals

Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1: Baseline Assessment (Month 1–2)
  • Phase 2: Global Benchmarking (Month 2–4)
  • Phase 3: Feasibility & Pilot Testing (Month 3–6)
  • Phase 4: Policy Drafting & Engagement (Month 6–8)
