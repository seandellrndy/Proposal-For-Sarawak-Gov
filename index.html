Project Phases

Phase 1: Research & Analysis (Month 1–2)

Study Taiwan's and others' technologies, regulations, and applications to establish a comprehensive foundation for the project.

Phase 2: Technology Assessment (Month 2–3)

Evaluate existing technologies and identify the most suitable approaches for Sarawak's industrial context and environmental conditions.

Phase 3: Feasibility Modelling & Pilot Testing (Month 3–6)

Conduct lab testing, Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), and small-scale product trials to validate technical feasibility and environmental impact.

Phase 4: Policy Drafting & Stakeholder Engagement (Month 6–8)

Develop reform recommendations, Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), and host stakeholder workshops to ensure comprehensive buy-in.

Conclusion

This proposal outlines a practical and cost-effective roadmap for turning steel and aluminium slag into a strategic asset for Sarawak. By leveraging Taiwan's model, Sarawak can protect the environment, boost local industry, and lead in industrial circular economy innovation.
